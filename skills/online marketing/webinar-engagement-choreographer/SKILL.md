## Step 2: Get Input

Ask the user:

- **Webinar script or outline**: Share the full script, slide deck, or at minimum a segment-by-segment outline with approximate timestamps. If unavailable, provide the topic, core offer, and intended runtime.
- **ICA profile**: Who is attending? What is their awareness level (problem-aware, solution-aware, product-aware)? What is their primary emotional state entering the webinar (curious, sceptical, desperate, hopeful)?
- **Platform & format**: Which webinar platform (Zoom, WebinarJam, EverWebinar, GoToWebinar, StreamYard, etc.)? Is this live, automated/evergreen, or hybrid? What interactive features are available (chat, polls, Q&A panel, reactions, hand-raise)?
- **Offer structure**: What is being sold, at what price point, and what is the primary call to action? Are there order bumps, downsells, or urgency mechanisms?
- **Historical performance** (if available): Current registration-to-show rate, show-through rate (% still present at offer), and conversion rate. Where do attendees typically drop off?
- **Any specific constraints**: Compliance restrictions (e.g., FTC, financial disclaimers), co-presenter logistics, or cultural considerations?

## Step 3: Segment the Webinar into Engagement Zones

Map the webinar timeline against the **Five-Zone Engagement Architecture**:

| Zone | Typical Timing | Strategic Purpose | Psychological Mechanism |
|------|---------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Zone 1: Arrival & Lock-In** | 0:00–0:07 | Confirm attendance was the right decision; establish interaction norms | Social proof, pattern interrupt, first micro-commitment |
| **Zone 2: Context & Pain Activation** | 0:07–0:20 | Surface the ICA's core frustration; create emotional stakes for staying | Empathy loops, problem amplification, future-pacing the cost of inaction |
| **Zone 3: Content & Belief Shifts** | 0:20–0:45 | Deliver the 3 secrets/frameworks; dismantle false beliefs | The 10-Minute Rule (re-engage every 10 min), epiphany bridging, teaching through story |
| **Zone 4: The Stack & Offer** | 0:45–1:00 | Transition to offer; build irresistible value perception | Trial closes, imagination exercises, price anchoring, commitment escalation |
| **Zone 5: Live Close & Q&A** | 1:00–1:20+ | Overcome objections in real time; create urgency | Fladlien's live Q&A selling, social proof stacking, scarcity mechanics |

Apply the **10-Minute Rule** rigorously: no segment exceeding 10 minutes without an explicit engagement beat that requires audience action. If any segment in the user's outline exceeds this threshold, flag it and insert an interaction point.

## Step 4: Design the Engagement Beat Sequence

For each zone, choreograph specific interaction elements from the following toolkit, selecting based on platform capabilities and ICA profile:

### 4a: Micro-Commitment Chat Prompts

Design a graduated commitment ladder using typed chat responses:

1. **Identity confirmations** (Zone 1): "Type where you're joining from!" → low-friction, establishes chat behaviour
2. **Problem acknowledgments** (Zone 2): "Type 'ME' if you've ever felt [specific ICA pain point]" → emotional investment
3. **Belief shift confirmations** (Zone 3): "Type 'AHA' if that framework just clicked for you" → cognitive commitment to new belief
4. **Trial closes** (Zone 4): "Type 'YES' if you'd want [specific outcome] even if [objection neutraliser]" → Brunson-style escalating agreement
5. **Decision declarations** (Zone 5): "Type 'I'M IN' when you've grabbed your spot" → public commitment, social proof cascade

Specify exact wording, placement (slide number or timestamp), and the presenter's verbal setup for each prompt. Each prompt must pass the **3-Second Rule**: attendees must be able to respond in under 3 seconds with no ambiguity.

### 4b: Polls & Interactive Elements

Design 2–4 polls strategically placed to:
- **Segment the audience** (Zone 1): Identify experience level, biggest challenge, or goal — enabling the presenter to tailor language in real time
- **Surface objections** (Zone 3): "What's your biggest concern about [approach]?" — allows pre-emptive objection handling
- **Gauge buying temperature** (Zone 4): "On a scale of 1–10, how ready are you to [desired transformation]?" — identifies fence-sitters for targeted Q&A

Provide exact poll questions, answer options (max 4 per poll), and how the presenter should narrate the results.

### 4c: Imagination Exercises

Script 2–3 guided imagination exercises using this structure:

1. **Setup**: Presenter says "I want you to close your eyes for just 10 seconds…" (pattern interrupt)
2. **Future-pace**: Vivid sensory description of life after the transformation — specific to ICA's desired outcome
3. **Contrast**: Brief return to current-state pain
4. **Bridge**: "The only difference between that vision and your reality right now is [the mechanism being taught/sold]"
5. **Re-engage**: Immediate chat prompt — "Type 'READY' if you could see that future"

Place these at: (1) end of Zone 2 to cement emotional stakes, (2) during the stack in Zone 4 to associate the offer with the imagined future, and optionally (3) during Q&A to re-anchor wavering attendees.

### 4d: Attention Reset Mechanisms

For each 10-minute segment, prescribe one of the following attention resets:

- **Pattern interrupt**: Unexpected slide (blank screen, single provocative word, contrarian statement)
- **Social proof drop**: Screenshot of result, live testimonial read, or "Let me show you what [name] achieved…"
- **Direct address**: "If you're still with me, drop a 🔥 in the chat — I want to make sure I'm not losing you"
- **Story open loop**: Begin a story, pause it — "I'll tell you how that ended in a moment, but first…"
- **Physical instruction**: "Grab a pen — you'll want to write this down" (kinaesthetic engagement)

## Step 5: Deliver the Engagement Choreography Map

Produce two deliverables:

### Deliverable 1: Webinar Engagement Timeline (table format)

| Timestamp | Zone | Slide/Segment | Engagement Beat Type | Exact Script/Prompt | Presenter Notes | Expected Audience Response |
|-----------|------|---------------|---------------------|---------------------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| 0:00 | 1 | Welcome | Chat prompt | "Type where you're joining from — city and country!" | Read responses aloud for 60s, name 5–6 cities | 60–80% participation |
| 0:03 | 1 | Promise frame | Poll | [specific poll] | Narrate results, use to validate the session topic | Establishes relevance |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

Complete the table for every engagement beat across the full webinar runtime. Target density: **minimum 1 engagement beat per 8 minutes** in Zones 1–3, **minimum 1 per 5 minutes** in Zones 4–5.

### Deliverable 2: Presenter Cue Sheet (condensed reference)

A single-page, print-ready reference document listing:
- Timestamp and engagement type (icon-coded: 💬 chat, 📊 poll, 🧠 imagination, ⚡ attention reset, ✅ trial close)
- The exact words to say (first 10 words of each prompt for quick glance)
- Platform action required (e.g., "Launch Poll #2", "Share screen: testimonial slide")

### Deliverable 3: Drop-Off Risk Assessment

Identify the 3 highest-risk drop-off points in the webinar based on:
- Segments exceeding 10 minutes without interaction
- Transitions between zones (especially the content-to-offer bridge)
- Technical friction points (platform switching, payment page redirect)

For each risk point, provide a specific countermeasure already built into the engagement timeline.
