## Step 2: Get Input

Ask the user for the following (adapt based on what FutureProof context already provides):

1. **Offer & Webinar Topic** — What are you selling at the end of this webinar? What is the teaching topic?
2. **ICA Profile** — Who is attending? What is their current situation, dominant frustration, and secret desire? (Pull from FutureProof context if available.)
3. **Your Backstory** — What is the personal experience or transformation that led you to this method/offer? Include: where you started, the low point, the turning point, and where you are now.
4. **The One Thing** — What is the single most important result or insight attendees will walk away with? (If unsure, we will define it together.)
5. **Selling Transparency Preference** — Are you comfortable telling attendees upfront that you will make an offer? (Fladlien method.) Rate comfort: 1–5.
6. **Existing Draft** — Do you have a current webinar opening or outline? If so, share it for baseline analysis.
7. **Performance Data** — Any metrics on current drop-off rates in the first 15 minutes, or audience feedback on previous openings?

## Step 3: Define 'The One Thing' Promise

Before writing a single word of story, lock the strategic anchor:

1. **Transformation Statement** — Articulate the before-state → after-state in one sentence using ICA language (not expert jargon). Format: *"By the end of this training, you will [specific outcome] — even if [their biggest objection/limiting belief]."*
2. **Specificity Audit** — Pressure-test the promise against five criteria:
   - **Measurable**: Can the attendee verify they received this?
   - **Time-bound**: Is it clear this happens *during* the webinar or within a defined period after?
   - **Belief-breaking**: Does it challenge an assumption the ICA currently holds?
   - **Desire-aligned**: Does it map to a goal the ICA already has (not one you need to create)?
   - **Offer-congruent**: Does the promise naturally ladder into the paid offer without logical gaps?
3. **Refine until all five criteria score YES.** Present the final Big Promise statement to the user for approval before proceeding.

## Step 4: Select the Origin Story Framework

Guide the user through framework selection using a diagnostic, not a guess:

### Framework A: Brunson's Epiphany Bridge
**Best when:**
- The user's transformation was driven by a single insight or "aha moment"
- The ICA's primary barrier is a **false belief** (they think the wrong thing is true)
- The offer is positioned as a new opportunity rather than an improvement on something they already do

**Structure (5 beats):**
1. **The Backstory** — Relatable starting point; same world as the ICA
2. **The Wall** — The external event or internal frustration that made the old path untenable
3. **The Epiphany** — The single insight that changed everything (must be concrete and transferable)
4. **The New Way** — What the user did differently as a direct result
5. **The Transformation** — Tangible, specific results (numbers, timeline, emotional shift)

### Framework B: Boyd's Conversion Story Formula
**Best when:**
- The user's journey involved a gradual or multi-step process rather than a single epiphany
- The ICA's primary barrier is **scepticism or distrust** (they have been burned before)
- The offer improves upon something the ICA is already doing (optimisation, not revolution)

**Structure (5 beats):**
1. **Reluctant Hero** — Establish credibility through vulnerability; "I didn't want to believe this either"
2. **The Defining Struggle** — Specific, detailed account of failure or stagnation (emotional weight)
3. **The Discovery Process** — How the method was found/built, emphasising trial and error (builds trust through honesty)
4. **The Proof Cascade** — Layer results: personal result → client/student result → broader validation
5. **The Invitation** — Bridge from story to teaching: "And that's exactly what I'm going to show you today"

Present both frameworks with the diagnostic criteria. Make a recommendation based on user inputs. Confirm selection before drafting.

## Step 5: Write the Full Webinar Opening (First 15 Minutes)

Draft the complete opening sequence in the following order. Each section must be written as a **speaker script** — exact words the presenter will say, with stage directions in brackets.

### Section 1: The Pattern Interrupt Hook (0:00–0:45)
- Open with a bold, specific claim or provocative question derived from The One Thing.
- Pattern: "[Unexpected statement]. And by the end of the next [X] minutes, you're going to see exactly why — and how to [apply it to their situation]."
- No pleasantries. No "welcome everyone." The first sentence earns the second.

### Section 2: Fladlien Selling Transparency (0:45–1:30)
- If user rated comfort ≥ 3: Write a direct, confident disclosure. Pattern: *"Full transparency — at the end of this training, I'm going to show you how we can work together on this. But here's my promise: even if you never spend a penny, what I teach in the next [X] minutes will be the most actionable session you attend this [week/month/year]."*
- If user rated comfort < 3: Write a softer version that implies an offer without explicit mention, preserving trust-building intent. Pattern: *"My only goal today is that you leave with [specific takeaway]. Everything I share is designed to be immediately useful — no fluff, no filler."*

### Section 3: Credential & Rapport Housekeeping (1:30–3:00)
- Brief bio structured as: **relevant result** → **who I've helped** → **why I care about this topic** (≤ 90 seconds spoken)
- Housekeeping items that double as engagement anchors: "Have your notebook ready — I'm going to give you [specific deliverable/framework name]." "Type your biggest [relevant challenge] in the chat — I'll address the top ones live."

### Section 4: The One Thing Setup (3:00–4:00)
- Restate the Big Promise.
- Introduce the agenda as a journey: "Here's what we'll cover: [3 bullet points mapping to the webinar body]. But everything hinges on [The One Thing], so let's start there."

### Section 5: The Origin Story (4:00–12:00)
- Write the full story using the selected framework (Epiphany Bridge or Conversion Story).
- **Sensory detail**: Include at least 3 moments of specific imagery (setting, emotion, dialogue).
- **Emotional pacing**: Map the story to a tension arc — start relatable, descend to the low point, inflect at the insight/discovery, ascend to transformation.
- **ICA Mirroring**: The backstory must contain at least 2 explicit parallels to the ICA's current situation so they think "this person was me."
- **Transition to Teaching**: Final paragraph bridges from story to content: "And that's exactly what led me to [The Framework/Method Name] — which is what I'm going to walk you through right now."

### Section 6: Re-hook & Content Bridge (12:00–15:00)
- Recap The One Thing promise.
- Ask an engagement question that primes for the first teaching section.
- Pattern: *"So here's what I need you to understand before we go any further: [restate core belief shift]. If you're with me on that, type 'YES' in the chat — because what I'm about to show you is going to [specific benefit]."*

## Step 6: Deliver Output

Produce a structured deliverable package:

1. **Webinar Opening Script (document)** — Complete speaker-ready script for the first 15 minutes, formatted with time stamps, stage directions, and chat-prompt cues. Delivered as a single document the user can paste into teleprompter software or print.
2. **Strategic Annotation Layer** — Inline annotations explaining *why* each section is structured the way it is (framework reference, psychological principle, ICA alignment rationale). Formatted as footnotes or a separate commentary document.
3. **Opening Scorecard** — Rate the draft against:
   - **Hook Strength** (1–10): Does the first sentence command attention?
   - **Transparency Trust Score** (1–10): Does the selling disclosure build or erode credibility?
   - **Story Emotional Arc** (1–10): Does the origin story hit all 5 beats with sensory and emotional weight?
   - **ICA Mirroring Accuracy** (1–10): Will the ICA see themselves in the story?
   - **Big Promise Clarity** (1–10): Is The One Thing unmistakable and compelling?
   - **Content Bridge Smoothness** (1–10): Does the transition from story to teaching feel inevitable?
4. **Critical Fixes List** — Top 3 highest-impact revisions if the user provides an existing draft (specific rewrites, not vague direction).
