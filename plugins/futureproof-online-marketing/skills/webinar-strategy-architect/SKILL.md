## Step 3: Classify the Offer Archetype

Before selecting a format, categorise the offer using the **Offer-Audience Matrix**:

| Dimension | Classification Options |
|---|---|
| **Price tier** | Low-ticket ($1–$297) · Mid-ticket ($297–$2,000) · High-ticket ($2,000–$10,000+) |
| **Offer complexity** | Simple (single deliverable, clear outcome) · Compound (multi-module, transformation arc) · Premium (bespoke, done-with-you or done-for-you) |
| **ICA sophistication** | Unaware / Problem-aware / Solution-aware / Product-aware / Most-aware |
| **Trust deficit** | Low (warm list, known brand) · Medium (mixed traffic) · High (cold traffic, sceptical market) |
| **Sales cycle** | Impulse (< 24 hrs) · Considered (1–7 days) · Consultative (7–30+ days, requires conversation) |

Document the classification explicitly — it drives every downstream decision.

## Step 4: Select the Optimal Webinar Format

Apply the **Format Decision Framework** drawing on Brunson's Perfect Webinar architecture, Erway's Mini Webinar methodology, and Porterfield's course-launch model:

### Format A — 90-Minute Live Webinar (Brunson Model)
**Select when:**
- Mid-ticket offers ($297–$2,000)
- ICA is problem-aware or solution-aware (needs belief-shifting)
- Trust deficit is medium to high
- Offer complexity is compound (requires reframing 3 false beliefs: vehicle, internal, external)
- Creator can commit to live delivery or simulated-live events

**Core mechanism:** Destroy and rebuild three false beliefs through origin story, vehicle framework, internal framework, and external framework before presenting the stack.

### Format B — 10–15 Minute Mini Webinar (Erway Model)
**Select when:**
- High-ticket offers ($2,000–$10,000+) selling to application or call
- ICA is solution-aware or product-aware (already buying, needs the right offer)
- Sales cycle is consultative (webinar's job is to pre-qualify and book calls, not close)
- Creator prioritises "sales first, then marketing" — proving the offer converts on calls before investing in long-form content
- Cold traffic or paid media is the primary acquisition channel

**Core mechanism:** Rapid authority positioning → single big idea → clear disqualification of alternatives → direct call-to-action to apply/book. No teaching. No slides. Personality-driven.

### Format C — Teach-Heavy Evergreen Webinar (Porterfield Model)
**Select when:**
- Low-to-mid-ticket course or membership ($47–$997)
- ICA is problem-aware (needs education to recognise the solution category)
- Audience is warm (existing list, social community) and expects value-first content
- Launch model is time-bound open/close cart with pre-launch runway
- Creator's brand positioning is educator/authority, not challenger/disruptor

**Core mechanism:** Deliver a genuine "quick win" teaching segment (30–40 min) that creates an epiphany, then position the full offer as the inevitable next step. Bridges teaching to selling through the "but there's more you need" gap.

### Format D — Hybrid / Sequenced (Advanced)
**Select when:**
- The Offer-Audience Matrix produces conflicting signals (e.g., high-ticket but unaware audience)
- Recommend a sequenced approach: e.g., Porterfield-style teaching webinar to warm cold traffic → Erway mini webinar retarget to book calls

**Document the rationale for the selected format with explicit reference to which matrix dimensions drove the decision.** If the user's instinct conflicts with the framework recommendation, flag the tension and explain the risk.

## Step 5: Architect the Big Promise & Offer Positioning

With the format locked, construct the three pillars:

### 5A — The Big Promise
Apply Brown's principle: *"The magic is in the message, not the medium."* The big promise must pass all four tests:

1. **Specificity test** — Contains a concrete outcome, not a vague aspiration ("Generate your first 3 paying clients in 30 days" vs. "Grow your business")
2. **Believability test** — Calibrated to ICA's current awareness level (bolder for product-aware, more grounded for problem-aware)
3. **Desirability test** — Addresses the #1 thing the ICA would pay to solve *right now*, not what they *should* want
4. **Uniqueness test** — Differentiates from the dominant narrative in the market via a unique mechanism or contrarian angle

Draft 3 candidate big promises ranked by projected resonance.

### 5B — Offer Architecture for the Webinar
Define how the offer will be presented within the chosen format:

- **For Format A (Brunson):** Full stack slide — core offer + 6 bonuses + guarantee + price anchor + urgency mechanism
- **For Format B (Erway):** Single compelling reason to get on a call — no full offer reveal, just enough to trigger application. Define the qualifying criteria.
- **For Format C (Porterfield):** Teaching-to-offer bridge — what is the "gap" between the quick win delivered and the full transformation? 3 bonuses max, fast-action incentive tied to cart close date.

### 5C — The One Belief
Identify the single core belief the ICA must hold by the end of the webinar for the offer to be a natural "yes." This is the north star for all content and scripting decisions downstream.

## Step 6: Deliver Output

Produce the **Webinar Strategy Blueprint** — a single reference document containing:

1. **Format Recommendation** — Selected format (A/B/C/D) with decision rationale matrix
2. **Offer-Audience Classification** — Completed matrix from Step 3
3. **Big Promise** — Top-ranked promise with runner-ups, including specificity/believability/desirability/uniqueness scoring (1–10 each)
4. **The One Belief** — Single sentence the entire webinar must install
5. **Offer Architecture** — Format-specific offer presentation structure
6. **Risk Flags** — Any misalignment between the user's stated preferences and framework recommendations (e.g., "You asked for a mini webinar, but your ICA is problem-unaware — this format will underperform without a pre-warming sequence")
7. **Recommended Next Steps** — Sequenced action items (e.g., "Step 1: Validate big promise via 5 ICA conversations. Step 2: Script the origin story. Step 3: Build slide deck using [format] template.")

Format as a structured document with clear headers, suitable for sharing with a marketing team, copywriter, or funnel builder.
