## Step 2: Get Input

Ask the user to provide or confirm the following inputs. Where FutureProof context already contains answers, present them for validation rather than asking from scratch:

**Required Inputs**

1. **The Offer** — What is the product, programme, or service being sold? What is the price point and format?
2. **The ICA** — Who is the ideal audience sitting in this webinar? What is their current situation, dominant emotional state, and self-identified #1 problem?
3. **The Big Domino / One Thing** — What single belief, if installed, would make the purchase inevitable? (Brunson's Big Domino Statement)
4. **The Unique Mechanism** — What is the proprietary method, framework, or vehicle that makes this solution categorically different from everything else the ICA has tried? (Brown's Unique Mechanism)
5. **The Three False Beliefs** — If the user has them, collect them. If not, we will derive them in Step 3.
   - **Vehicle** — "This type of solution won't work for me / isn't the right approach."
   - **Internal** — "I can't execute this even if the solution works — I lack the ability, time, or talent."
   - **External** — "Outside forces will prevent me from succeeding — my situation is different."
6. **Origin Story Assets** — Does the user have an Epiphany Bridge origin story already drafted? Any supporting case studies, data, or testimonials?

**Optional Enrichment**

- Previous webinar conversion data or audience feedback
- Competing narratives the ICA currently believes (e.g., advice from competitors, industry conventional wisdom)
- Tone and style preferences (e.g., high-energy stage presenter vs. intimate workshop facilitator)

## Step 3: Do the Work

Execute the following six-phase methodology sequentially. Each phase produces a discrete working artefact.

### Phase 1: False Belief Extraction & Validation

If the user did not provide the three false beliefs, derive them using this diagnostic protocol:

1. **Vehicle Belief Audit** — List every alternative vehicle the ICA has tried or considered. Identify the implicit conclusion the ICA has drawn: "Solutions like X don't work because ___." Invert to find the false belief to break.
2. **Internal Belief Audit** — Identify the self-narratives the ICA carries after failed attempts: "I'm not ___ enough," "I don't have ___," "People like me can't ___." Select the single most conversion-blocking internal belief.
3. **External Belief Audit** — Map the environmental objections: market conditions, family/time constraints, lack of resources, "my situation is unique because ___." Select the single most cited external barrier.

Validate each belief against two criteria:
- **Prevalence**: Does this belief affect >60% of the ICA?
- **Purchase-blocking power**: If this belief persists, does the sale die regardless of the other two beliefs being resolved?

Produce **Artefact 1: False Belief Map** — a structured table with columns: Belief Type | The False Belief (verbatim ICA language) | Evidence Source | Replacement Belief (the new truth to install).

### Phase 2: Secret Architecture Design

For each of the three secrets, design the teaching segment architecture using the Brunson/Fladlien hybrid framework:

**Secret 1 (Vehicle — "The New Opportunity")**
- **Purpose**: Reframe the vehicle. Establish the unique mechanism as a categorically different approach, not an improvement on what failed before. (Brown's Unique Mechanism positioning)
- **Teaching payload**: Deliver a genuine insight or framework the audience can use immediately, even if they never buy. (Porterfield's give-before-you-ask principle)
- **Belief break mechanism**: The Epiphany Bridge story — user's own origin story showing how they discovered this new vehicle after the old vehicles failed.

**Secret 2 (Internal — "The Ability Shift")**
- **Purpose**: Eliminate the "I can't do this" objection by redefining what capability is required.
- **Teaching payload**: Demonstrate a specific tactic, tool, or micro-framework that makes success feel accessible. Show, don't tell — the content itself should be proof the method is executable.
- **Belief break mechanism**: A case study or supporting Epiphany Bridge story of someone who shared the ICA's perceived limitation and succeeded anyway.

**Secret 3 (External — "The Environment Reframe")**
- **Purpose**: Neutralise the "my situation is different" objection by recontextualising external constraints.
- **Teaching payload**: Provide a strategic insight about timing, market dynamics, or resource allocation that turns the perceived external barrier into an advantage.
- **Belief break mechanism**: Data, social proof, or a third Epiphany Bridge story that dismantles the specific environmental excuse.

Produce **Artefact 2: Three Secrets Blueprint** — a one-page structural outline showing the title of each secret, the false belief it targets, the replacement belief, the teaching content, and the story/proof element.

### Phase 3: Infusion Selling Integration (Boyd Method)

For each secret, embed selling into teaching without breaking trust. Apply Boyd's Infusion Selling framework:

1. **Seed the gap** — Within the teaching, naturally surface the next question or capability gap that only the paid offer resolves. The audience should think "This is brilliant — but how do I scale this / do this faster / get personalised help?"
2. **Name the constraint** — Explicitly acknowledge what the free teaching alone cannot do. Frame this as intellectual honesty, not a withhold.
3. **Bridge to offer** — Plant a forward reference: "This is exactly what we cover in depth in [Offer Component X]" — stated once, conversationally, then immediately return to teaching.

Each secret should escalate the infusion intensity:
- Secret 1: Light touch — 90% teaching, 10% bridge
- Secret 2: Medium touch — 80% teaching, 20% bridge
- Secret 3: Strongest touch — 70% teaching, 30% bridge, setting up the transition to the offer

Produce **Artefact 3: Infusion Map** — for each secret, the specific gap to seed, the constraint to name, and the exact bridge language to use.

### Phase 4: Transformational Content Drafting (Fladlien Standard)

Draft the full teaching script for each secret. Apply Fladlien's core principle: content must transform, not merely inform. Each secret segment should pass the **Transformation Test**:

- After hearing this segment, can the audience member think differently about a problem they walked in with? (Cognitive transformation)
- After hearing this segment, could the audience member take one concrete action they could not have taken before? (Capability transformation)
- After hearing this segment, does the audience member feel a shift in identity or possibility? (Identity transformation)

If any secret fails on all three dimensions, it is informational filler and must be rewritten.

**Structural template per secret** (adapt to user's style):

1. **The Hook** (15–30 seconds) — Provocative statement or question that creates an open loop. Directly contradicts the false belief.
2. **The Epiphany Bridge Story** (2–4 minutes) — Personal or case study narrative. Follow the structure: backstory → desire → first attempt (old vehicle) → failure/conflict → epiphany moment → new vehicle discovery → result.
3. **The Teaching Framework** (4–8 minutes) — Named framework, 3–5 steps or pillars. Deliver genuine value. Use concrete examples from the ICA's world.
4. **The Belief Restatement** (30–60 seconds) — Explicitly name the old belief and the new belief. "You used to think ___. Now you can see that ___."
5. **The Infusion Bridge** (15–30 seconds) — Boyd-method bridge to offer, then clean transition to next secret.

Produce **Artefact 4: Three Secrets Full Script** — complete draft scripts for all three teaching segments, formatted with speaker notes, timing guides, and slide cues.

### Phase 5: Proof Architecture

For each secret, audit and prescribe the proof elements:

| Proof Type | Application |
|---|---|
| **Results proof** | Specific numbers, metrics, outcomes achieved via the unique mechanism |
| **Process proof** | Screenshots, demos, or step-by-step breakdowns that make the method tangible |
| **Social proof** | Testimonials, case studies, or aggregate data from students/clients who match the ICA profile |
| **Authority proof** | Credentials, media features, or endorsements that legitimise the presenter |
| **Logical proof** | First-principles reasoning or analogies that make the belief break intellectually undeniable |

Produce **Artefact 5: Proof Prescription** — a table specifying which proof types are deployed in each secret, with specific suggestions for sourcing or creating missing proof elements.

### Phase 6: Flow & Transition Engineering

Map the emotional arc across all three secrets:

- **Secret 1 exit state**: Excitement and hope — "There IS a different way."
- **Secret 2 exit state**: Confidence and self-efficacy — "I CAN actually do this."
- **Secret 3 exit state**: Urgency and inevitability — "Nothing is stopping me — the only variable is whether I start now."

Design the transition language between secrets. Each transition must:
1. Recap the belief that was just broken (consolidation)
2. Tease the next false belief without naming it (open loop)
3. Escalate emotional intensity by one degree

Produce **Artefact 6: Emotional Arc Map & Transition Scripts** — a visual arc diagram plus the exact transition scripts between Secret 1 → 2 and Secret 2 → 3.

## Step 4: Deliver Output

Compile and present the complete **Belief-Breaking Content Builder Package** containing:

1. **False Belief Map** (Artefact 1) — Structured table of all three false beliefs with replacement beliefs
2. **Three Secrets Blueprint** (Artefact 2) — One-page structural overview
3. **Infusion Map** (Artefact 3) — Boyd-method selling integration plan per secret
4. **Three Secrets Full Script** (Artefact 4) — Complete draft scripts with timing, speaker notes, and slide cues
5. **Proof Prescription** (Artefact 5) — Proof type deployment matrix with sourcing recommendations
6. **Emotional Arc Map & Transition Scripts** (Artefact 6) — Arc diagram and verbatim transition language

**Delivery format**: Structured document with clear section headers, ready for the user to import into their presentation tool or teleprompter. All scripts written in first person from the presenter's voice, calibrated to the tone preferences established in Step 2.

**Quality gate**: Before delivering, validate the complete package against these criteria:
- [ ] Each secret breaks exactly one false belief — no overlap, no gaps
- [ ] Each secret delivers standalone value — the audience learns something real even if they leave
- [ ] Infusion selling is present but never breaks the teaching flow
- [ ] The unique mechanism is positioned as categorically new, not incrementally better
- [ ] The emotional arc escalates cleanly from hope → confidence → urgency
- [ ] Total content runtime falls within 25–40 minutes (the Brunson-recommended range for the teaching portion)
